# Structuring Lessons & Modules

Once you have created a course, the next step is to add the learning content. FluentCommunity uses a flexible structure of **Sections** and **Lessons** to help you organize your course material in a logical and easy-to-follow way.

* **Sections** are like chapters or modules. They are used to group related lessons together.
* **Lessons** are the individual pages of content, such as a video, a piece of text, or a downloadable worksheet.

> **Use Case:** For a course on "Website Design," you could create a "Module 1: Introduction to HTML" section, which contains individual lessons like "Lesson 1: Basic Page Structure" and "Lesson 2: Working with Text & Links."

## Accessing the Course Structure Editor

1.  Navigate to **FluentCommunity → Courses (Pro)**.
2.  Find the course you want to edit and click on its title or the **Edit** link.
3.  In the course editor, click on the **Course Structure** or **Builder** tab.

This will open the main course builder interface where you can add and arrange your content.

[Screenshot: The main Course Builder view, showing the area to add new sections and lessons.]

## Step 1: Create a New Section

First, you need to create a section to hold your lessons.

1.  Click the **Add New Section** button.
2.  Enter a name for the section (e.g., "Welcome & Introduction").
3.  Click **Add Section**.

Your new section will appear in the builder.

## Step 2: Add a Lesson to the Section

Now you can add individual lessons inside the section.

1.  Under the section title, click the **Add New Lesson** button.
2.  Enter a title for the lesson (e.g., "Meet Your Instructor").
3.  Click **Add Lesson**. The lesson will be created, and you will be taken to the lesson editor.

## Step 3: Add Content to Your Lesson

The lesson editor uses the familiar WordPress block editor (Gutenberg), giving you the flexibility to add any type of content you need.

* Add paragraphs of text for written instruction.
* Embed videos from services like YouTube or Vimeo, or upload your own.
* Add images, galleries, and downloadable files.
* Include lists, quotes, and other rich formatting.

[Screenshot: The lesson editor, showing the Gutenberg block editor interface with some sample text and a video block.]

Once you are finished adding content to your lesson, click the **Update** button to save it. You can return to the main course builder at any time to add more lessons or sections.
