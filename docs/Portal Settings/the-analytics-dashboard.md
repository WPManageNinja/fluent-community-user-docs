# The Analytics Dashboard

Understanding the health and growth of your community is crucial for making informed decisions. The **Analytics Dashboard** provides a comprehensive overview of your community's most important metrics, all in one place.

> **Use Case:** You are running a month-long engagement campaign to encourage more discussions. You can use the Analytics Dashboard to monitor the number of new posts and comments each day, track which members are participating the most, and determine if your campaign is successfully boosting activity.

## How to Access the Analytics Dashboard

1.  From your WordPress dashboard, navigate to **FluentCommunity → Analytics (Pro)**.
2.  This will open the main analytics interface, which is composed of various reports and charts.

[Screenshot: The main Analytics Dashboard view, showing several charts and key performance indicators (KPIs).]

## Key Metrics You Can Track

The dashboard provides several key insights into your community's performance.

* **Member Growth:** View a graph that shows how your total member count has grown over time. You can typically filter this by different date ranges (e.g., last 30 days, last 90 days).
* **Engagement Statistics:** See a summary of core engagement actions, including the total number of posts, comments, and reactions within a specific period.
* **Top Active Spaces:** Identify which of your Spaces are generating the most discussion. This report shows a ranked list of Spaces based on the number of new posts and comments.
* **Top Active Users:** See a list of your most engaged members. This report ranks users based on their activity, such as the number of posts and comments they have contributed.

> **✨ Note:**
> The data presented in the Analytics Dashboard is invaluable for your community strategy. Use the "Top Active Spaces" report to understand what topics your members are most interested in, and use the "Top Active Users" report to identify potential community moderators or advocates you can reward.

By regularly reviewing these analytics, you can get a clear, data-driven picture of what's working in your community and where you might need to focus your efforts to stimulate growth and engagement.
