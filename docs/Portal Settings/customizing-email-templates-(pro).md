# Customizing Email Templates (Pro)

In addition to on-site notifications, FluentCommunity sends automated emails to your members for key events. The Pro version allows you to customize the content and branding of these emails to ensure they match your community's identity perfectly.

> **Use Case:** You can edit the "New Member Welcome" email to include a personal message from the founder, add links to your "Getting Started" guide, and change the colors and logo to match your website's branding.

## How to Access Email Templates

1.  From your WordPress dashboard, navigate to **FluentCommunity → Settings**.
2.  Click on the **Emails** or **Email Notifications** tab.
3.  Here you will find a list of all the email templates used by the plugin.

[Screenshot: The main Email Settings screen, showing a list of different email templates like "Welcome Email," "New Post Notification," etc.]

## Customizing an Email Template

You can edit each email template individually.

1.  Click the **Edit** or **Customize** button next to the template you want to change (e.g., "Notification of a New Reply").
2.  This will open the email editor. You can typically customize the following elements:
    * **Email Subject:** Change the subject line of the email. You can often use dynamic placeholders (like `{{user_name}}` or `{{post_title}}`) to personalize it.
    * **Email Body:** Modify the main content of the email using a rich text editor. You can rewrite the text, add images, and use placeholders to include dynamic information.
    * **Branding:** You may have options to change the header image, colors, and footer text to match your brand.

[Screenshot: The email template editor, showing the fields for the subject line and the main body content with formatting options.]

> **✨ Note:**
> Using dynamic placeholders is key to creating personalized and effective email notifications. Look for a guide or a list of available placeholders within the editor to see what data you can insert automatically.

After making your changes, click the **Save Settings** or **Update Template** button. All future emails of that type sent from your community will now use your new custom version.

## User Email Preferences

It's important to remember that individual members can also control their own email preferences from their profile settings. They can choose to opt-out of certain non-essential notifications. Your customizations will apply to the emails they *do* choose to receive.
