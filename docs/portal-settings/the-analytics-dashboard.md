# The Analytics Dashboard

Guessing how your community is doing is exhausting. The **Analytics** report answers it with numbers: how many people joined, how much they posted, who is doing the talking, and which spaces are actually alive.

Everything lives on one screen, split into three tabs **Overview**, **Members**, and **Spaces**.

> **Use Case:** You are running a month-long campaign to spark more discussion. Set the date range to that month, watch the **Posts** and **Comments** numbers on the Overview tab, then check **Top Members** to see who responded to the push — and reward them.

## How to Open Analytics

1. Open your community portal and click **Settings** at the bottom of the left sidebar.
2. In **Portal Settings**, scroll down to the **Reports** section.
3. Click **Analytics**.

The page opens on the **Overview** tab, labeled **Reports (Beta)** at the top.

## Filtering What You See

Two controls sit at the top-right of every tab and apply to everything below them:

* **Select** — a quick preset for the reporting period.
* **Date range** — a calendar picker for an exact start and end date.

The **Spaces** tab adds a third control, **Select Space**, so you can narrow the whole report down to a single space instead of the entire community.

> **💡 Tip:** Most stat cards show a small green badge like `+65 %` next to the number. That is the change compared with the previous period of the same length — so a 30-day range is compared against the 30 days before it. Widen or shorten the range and that comparison moves with it.

## The Overview Tab

This is your community at a glance.

![The Overview tab of the FluentCommunity Analytics report, showing stat cards, the Activities chart, and the Popular day and time table](/images/portal-settings/the-analytics-dashboard/analytics-overview.webp)

**Stat cards.** Four totals for the selected period — **Members**, **Posts**, **Comments**, and **Spaces** — each with its change badge.

**Activities chart.** A combined chart with a dropdown in its top-right corner so you can switch the metric being plotted (Posts, and other activity types). It draws two things at once:

* **Bars** — how much happened in each individual period.
* **Cumulative line** — the running total over time.

The bars tell you where the spikes were. The line tells you whether the community is still climbing or has flattened out.

**Popular day & time.** A grid of time blocks (12:00 AM–3:59 AM, 4:00 AM–7:59 AM, and so on) across Monday to Sunday, with the activity count in each cell. The heaviest cells are the hours your members are actually online — which is exactly when your announcements and new posts should go out.

## The Members Tab

Everything about the people in your community.

![The Members tab showing member stat cards, the Members Activities chart, and the Top Members, Post starters, and Top Commenters lists](/images/portal-settings/the-analytics-dashboard/analytics-members.webp)

**Stat cards:**

| Card | What it counts |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Total Members** | Everyone in the community for the selected period. |
| **Active Members** | Members who actually did something — posted, commented, or reacted. |
| **New Members** | People who joined during the selected period. |
| **Pending Members** | Join requests waiting on your approval. |

**Members Activities chart.** Same shape as the Overview chart — bars for activity per period, a cumulative line for the running total — but scoped to member signups and member activity.

**The three leaderboards** at the bottom are the most useful part of this tab:

* **Top Members** — your most active people overall.
* **Post starters** — the members who begin conversations rather than just joining them.
* **Top Commenters** — the members who keep threads going.

Each row shows the member's avatar, name, how long ago they joined, and their count for that list.

> **✨ Note:**
> Post starters and Top Commenters are two different kinds of valuable. Post starters generate the content; Top Commenters generate the replies that make a community feel busy. Look for names that appear in both — those are your best candidates for [space managers](/assigning-managers) or moderators.

## The Spaces Tab

Which parts of your community are thriving, and which are quiet.

![The Spaces tab showing space stat cards, the Space Activities chart, and the Popular Spaces table](/images/portal-settings/the-analytics-dashboard/analytics-spaces.webp)

**Stat cards.** **Total Spaces**, **Total Posts**, **Total Comments**, and **Total Members** for the selected period — or for one space, if you picked one in the **Select Space** dropdown.

**Space Activities chart.** Activity bars plus the cumulative line, showing how space activity has built up over the range.

**Popular Spaces table.** A ranked breakdown with one row per space and columns for **Space Name**, **Posts**, **Comments**, and **Members**.

That last table is worth reading carefully. A space with plenty of members but almost no posts is not really working — it usually needs a prompt, a pinned discussion, or to be merged into a busier space. See [Creating a New Space](/creating-a-new-space) and [Space Settings & Privacy](/space-settings-&-privacy) for your options.

> **⚠️ Important:**
> Analytics is labeled **Reports (Beta)**. The layout and the available metrics may change in future FluentCommunity releases, so treat the numbers as directional insight rather than a permanent audit record.

## Turning the Numbers Into Action

| What you see | What to try |
| :--- | :--- |
| New Members climbing, Active Members flat | People are arriving but not engaging — improve onboarding and your welcome post. |
| One time block dominates Popular day & time | Schedule announcements and new discussions for that window. |
| A few names own every leaderboard | Promote them to moderators, or use [badges and points](/creating-&-assigning-badges) to pull more members in. |
| Several spaces with zero posts | Consolidate them, or seed each with a starter discussion. |

Check in on these reports on a regular rhythm — weekly or monthly — rather than daily. Community trends are slow, and a longer date range tells you far more than yesterday's numbers.
