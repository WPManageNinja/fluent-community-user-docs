# Understanding The Module System

One of the core design principles of FluentCommunity is performance. To ensure your community site remains fast and efficient, we built it on a **modular system**. This means you can enable only the major features (modules) you actually need, without loading unnecessary code for features you don't use.

> **Use Case:** If you are building a simple discussion forum without any courses or points, you can disable the **Courses** and **Gamification** modules. This ensures that your site's resources are not used to load these features in the background, resulting in a faster experience for your members.



## How to Access the Module System

You can manage your modules at any time from the main FluentCommunity settings.

1.  From your WordPress dashboard, navigate to **FluentCommunity → Settings**.
2.  Click on the **Modules** tab.

    [Screenshot: The FluentCommunity settings page with the "Modules" tab highlighted.]



## Available Core Modules

On the Modules screen, you will see a list of the major features that can be toggled on or off.

[Screenshot: A view of the available modules, like Gamification and Courses, with their toggle switches.]

The primary modules include:

* **Gamification (Pro):** This module controls all features related to points, badges, and leaderboards. If you don't plan to reward users for engagement, you can keep this disabled.
* **Courses (Pro):** This module controls the entire Learning Management System (LMS). If your community is purely for discussions and not for e-learning, you can disable this to save resources.

To enable or disable a module, simply click the toggle switch next to its name. The change is saved and takes effect immediately.

> **✨ Note:**
> Some features, like **File & Document Library**, may also be controlled from this screen depending on your version and configuration. It's always a good idea to check here first when you're looking to enable a major feature.
